FIDES Ecosystem Explorer — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 17 June 2026

1. Who we are

This service is operated by FIDES Labs BV, Vredenburg 40, 3511 BD Utrecht, the Netherlands ("we", "us"), the data controller for the processing described here. For any privacy question or to exercise your rights, contact us at privacy@fides.community.

This policy concerns the processing of personal data. The use of cookies and similar tracking technologies on the website is described separately in our Cookie Policy, where you can also manage your consent.

This policy applies to:

  • the FIDES website at fides.community, including its contact form and optional sign-in;
  • the public, read-only catalog APIs served at api.fides.community (credential types, organizations, issuers, wallets, relying parties);
  • the AI connector (MCP server) at api.fides.community/api/mcp, which AI assistants (e.g. ChatGPT, Claude) use to query the FIDES catalogs;
  • the conversational assistant offered on the FIDES website.

2. In short

  • The FIDES catalogs and APIs are an open, read-only directory; no account or login is required to browse or query them.
  • The website separately offers an optional sign-in and a contact form, which do involve some personal data (see sections 3d and 3e).
  • We do not sell personal data, and we do not use the catalogs/APIs to profile individuals.
  • We keep only the minimal data needed to run, secure and improve the service and to respond to you.
  • When you use the conversational assistant, the text of your question is sent to an AI language model provider to generate an answer (see section 5).
  • Cookies and tracking on the website are covered by our separate Cookie Policy.

3. What information we process

a. Catalog content (public). The catalogs contain information about organizations and their products in the digital-identity ecosystem (names, websites, logos, supported standards, etc.). This is published by the organizations themselves or compiled from public sources, and may include business contact details. It is intended to be public and is not used to profile individuals.

b. Technical and usage data. When you (or an AI assistant acting on your behalf) call our API or MCP endpoint, our servers and infrastructure provider automatically process standard request data: IP address, timestamp, the endpoint or tool requested and its parameters, response status, latency, and user-agent. We use this to operate the service, ensure security, apply rate-limiting, and prevent abuse.

c. Conversational assistant input. If you use the assistant, we process the text you type and the queries derived from it in order to answer your question. We do not ask for and do not need personal data to answer; please do not enter sensitive personal information in the chat.

d. Contact form. If you contact us through the website form, we process the email address you provide and the content of your message (and any other details you choose to include) so that we can read and respond to your enquiry.

e. Sign-in. The website offers an optional sign-in using your email address. We verify your email by sending a confirmation link, which is handled by our own service (no third-party identity verifier is involved). We process your email address to authenticate you and to associate your contributions — such as "likes", and in future voting on community awards — with your account. You can delete your account at any time, which removes the associated data.

f. Cookies and website analytics. The website uses functional, statistics and (optional) marketing cookies, including privacy-friendly analytics (Matomo) and embedded content. These are only placed in line with your choices and are described in detail, with the option to manage consent, in our Cookie Policy. We do not use your data for cross-site advertising profiling or for automated decision-making with legal effects. The catalog APIs and the MCP connector (api.fides.community) set no cookies; outside the contact form, sign-in and the cookies described above, browsing requires no personal data.

4. Why we process it, and our legal basis (GDPR)

  • To provide the service (return catalog data, run the assistant) — necessary for our legitimate interest in offering an open ecosystem directory, and, for the assistant, to perform the action you request.
  • To respond to your enquiries (contact form) — your consent and/or our legitimate interest in handling your request.
  • To authenticate you and attribute your contributions (sign-in) — necessary to provide the sign-in/community feature you choose to use.
  • To secure and maintain the service (rate-limiting, abuse prevention, debugging, reliability) — our legitimate interest in keeping the service safe and available.
  • We do not rely on your data for marketing or automated decision-making with legal effects.

5. Who receives the data (processors and third parties)

  • Hosting / infrastructure: the website, APIs, gateway and catalog backends are hosted on Vercel Inc. (gateway/catalog APIs) and the website's hosting provider, which process request data on our behalf as our hosting processors.
  • AI language model providers (assistant only): when you use the conversational assistant, your question is transmitted to OpenAI (United States) and/or Mistral AI (France, EU), which process it to generate a response. We configure these providers so that your inputs are not used to train their models.
  • Contact form: form submissions are processed through our website's form tool (WPForms) and emailed/stored within our WordPress site. Google reCAPTCHA is used on forms for spam prevention.
  • Sign-in: email verification (the confirmation link) is handled by our own service; no third-party identity verifier is involved.
  • Website analytics & embeds: privacy-friendly analytics (Matomo) and embedded third-party content (e.g. video, social) as listed in our Cookie Policy.
  • AI assistants you choose to connect (MCP): if you add our MCP connector to a third-party assistant (e.g. ChatGPT, Claude), that assistant sends requests to our endpoint on your behalf. Your use of that assistant is governed by that provider's own privacy policy.
  • We do not sell, rent, or trade personal data, and we do not share it for third-party marketing.

6. International transfers

Some processors may process data outside the European Economic Area. Our hosting and OpenAI are US-based; Mistral AI is EU-based. Where personal data is transferred outside the EEA, we rely on appropriate safeguards such as the EU Standard Contractual Clauses and/or an applicable adequacy framework.

7. How long we keep it

  • Technical/usage logs: retained for a limited period for security and operations — typically up to 30 days — after which they are deleted or aggregated into non-identifying statistics.
  • Conversational assistant input: processed transiently to answer your question and not stored as a long-term conversation history by us; short-lived operational logs may briefly contain request data as described above.
  • Contact form messages: retained for as long as needed to handle your enquiry and for a reasonable period afterwards, then deleted.
  • Sign-in / account data: retained for as long as your account remains active; deleted when you delete your account or on request.
  • Catalog content: retained for as long as the entry is part of the public catalog.

8. Your rights

Under the GDPR you have the right to request access to, rectification of, erasure of, or restriction of processing of your personal data, to object to processing, and to data portability. If you have an account, you can also delete it yourself at any time. To exercise any other right, email privacy@fides.community. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection supervisory authority.

For anonymous browsing we may be unable to identify you from technical logs alone; for the contact form and sign-in we can locate your data via the email address you provided.

9. Security

We serve all traffic over HTTPS, expose only read-only endpoints for the catalogs/APIs, apply rate-limiting and origin controls on the AI connector, and store no passwords (sign-in uses an email confirmation link, not a password). No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, but we take appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect the data we process.

10. Children

The service is intended for professional and general audiences and is not directed at children.
